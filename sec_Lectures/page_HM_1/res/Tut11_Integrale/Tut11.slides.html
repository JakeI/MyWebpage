Integralrechnung¶

$$ \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-x^2} = \sqrt{\pi} $$

Eigenschaften des Riemannschen Integrals:¶

\begin{align} & \int_a^b f(x) \, dx = F(x)\big|_a^b &=&\; \big[ F(x) \big]_a^b = F(b) - F(a) & \text{mit} \; F(x) := \int f(x) \, dx \\ & \int_a^b f(x) \, dx + \int_b^c f(x) \,dx &=&\; \int_a^c f(x) \,dx & \\ & \int_a^b f(x) \,dx &=&\; -\int_b^a f(x) \,dx & \\ \text{Linearitt:} \; \; \; & \int c \big( f(x) + g(x) \big) \,dx &=&\; c \bigg(\int f(x) \,dx + \int g(x) \,dx \bigg) & \\ \text{Substitution:}\; \; \; & \int_{u(a)}^{u(b)} f(u) \,du &=&\; \int_a^b f\big(u(x)\big) u'(x) \,dx & \text{bzw.} \; \int f \,du = \int f(u) u' \,dx \\ \text{Partiell:} \; \; \; & \int_a^b u(x) v'(x) \,dx &=&\; u(x) v(x) \big|_a^b - \int_a^b v(x) u'(x) \,dx & \text{bzw.} \; \int u \,dv = uv - \int v \,du \end{align}
